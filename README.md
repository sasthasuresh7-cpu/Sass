Mitigation for High Risk Business – Real Estate:
Although the counterparty operates in the real estate sector, which is generally considered a higher-risk industry from an AML perspective due to the potential for misuse in layering and asset placement activities, the reviewed transactions appear consistent with legitimate rental or lease-related payments. The payment narrations explicitly reference “lease” and “rental fees,” establishing a clear economic purpose for the transactions. Further, the transaction pattern demonstrates consistency in beneficiary relationship, payment frequency, and transaction values without indicators of unusual structuring, rapid movement of funds, or involvement of unrelated third parties. No adverse media, sanctions exposure, or suspicious transactional behavior was identified during the review. Accordingly, the business risk associated with the real estate sector appears reasonably mitigated in the context of the identified rental arrangement and overall transaction activity.

Given the customer’s continued association with YOCEAN and the previously identified adverse risk indicators, the current activity warrants escalation for further review to assess potential money laundering and concealment of illicit proceeds.
